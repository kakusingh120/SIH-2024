Nagpur / Maharashtra

Futala Lake is a lake in Nagpur in the Indian state Maharashtra. The lake covers 60 acres. Built by the Bhosle kings of Nagpur, the lake is known for its col fountains. In the evenings the site is illuminated with halogen lights and Tanga (carriage) rides. The la surrounded on three sides by forest and a landscaped beach on the fourth side.

Beautification of Futala Lake:

Quality over quantity.

        Futala Lake, a 200-year-old landmark in western Nagpur, underwent a major beautification project starting in 2003, funded by NIT and the state government. The work involved large-scale desilting, lotus removal, and improvements such as an eastern guard wall, granite-paved pathways, widened roads, parking bays, decorative lamps, benches, and high mast lighting. Fountains were installed, and an Immersion Ghat with ramps was built. In phase two, a 1,300-meter road was paved, addressing parking issues near the Hanuman Temple. Though much has been completed, upcoming plans include hill steps, sand beach paving, and a Nana-Nani park. In 2010, NIT partnered with Sell Ads for lake maintenance and food kiosk development under a 5-year BOT agreement.

Aquatic life

You wanna see.

        The nutrient-rich environment of Futala Lake has fostered the abundant growth of diverse aquatic plants like water hyacinth, water lily, hydrilla, wolffia, and potamogeton, along with vibrant algae. These flourishing ecosystems contribute to the lake's natural biodiversity and add to its scenic beauty.
